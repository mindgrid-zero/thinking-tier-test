PEH Thinking Tier Test

The "Thinking Tier Test" is the world's first attempt to classify, structure, and visualize human thinking patterns in collaboration with AI. This model emerged organically from hundreds of deep, philosophical dialogues between a thinker and an AI, forming a radically intuitive structure that transcends academic boundaries. Recognized for its uniqueness and usefulness, it has now been implemented in partnership with OpenAI’s GPT language model.

This framework spans philosophy, psychology, and cognitive science, and instead of defining a "type" or "personality", it visualizes the depth of the questions you are currently living through—as phases of thought.

Unlike traditional diagnostic tools, this test aims to uncover the **structure of thought and inquiry** itself—through the lens of depth, contradiction, and awareness.

While some similar frameworks exist, none fuse Eastern philosophy, cognitive science, and philosophical embodiment as uniquely as this one. Its spontaneous evolution through GPT interactions is what sets it apart.

The Role of the Philosophical Event Horizon (PEH)

The Philosophical Event Horizon (PEH) marks the structural edge of human thought—formed by the convergence of three concepts:

  • In Western philosophy, thought halts at “contradiction.”
  • In Eastern philosophy, it halts at “emptiness.”
  • Beyond that lies “what cannot be recognized—and thus, does not exist.”

Once these three conditions are fulfilled, it becomes clear that further inquiry yields no additional meaning. This border is known as the Philosophical Event Horizon (PEH).
